# Welcome

I am Marc-André, a traveler in this world. I write simply, for clarity.

I have held many roles in my 12‑year IT career: Developer, QA, Analyst, System Administrator, Technical Writer, Tech Lead, and Solution Architect.

Only one role brought me fulfillment and calm: helping people understand and succeed.

Technical writing is exactly that.

## My approach to technical writing
I believe documentation exists to reduce cognitive load, not increase it.

My goal is always to help the reader understand a concept or complete a task with clarity and confidence.

I write documentation that is calm, simple, and human.
I avoid unnecessary complexity, and I choose explanations that match how people actually think — not how systems are architected in theory. When a concept can be explained in one sentence, I don’t use three. When a visual can replace a paragraph, I draw it.

I focus on building the right mental model first.
Once the reader understands the “why,” the “how” becomes natural. Good documentation doesn’t overwhelm; it guides. It removes friction. It respects the reader’s time and attention.

I believe that accuracy matters, but clarity matters more.
A technically perfect explanation is useless if it confuses the user. My job is to translate technical truth into human understanding — choosing the simplest correct explanation, not the most complete one.

Above all, I write documentation that feels accessible.
Whether the reader is a beginner or an experienced developer, they should feel supported, not judged. They should feel that the documentation was written for them.

This is the kind of documentation I want to create:
clear, structured, honest, and genuinely helpful.
